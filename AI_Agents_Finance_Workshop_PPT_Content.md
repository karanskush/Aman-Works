### **SLIDE 7 — Anatomy of an AI Agent** *(2 min)*

**Headline:** What Makes an Agent Different from a Bot or a Copilot?

**Visual: Circular diagram with 4 capabilities:**

1. **SENSE** — Monitors data streams continuously (bank feeds, invoices, GL postings)
2. **REASON** — Applies business logic, identifies anomalies, weighs options (not just pattern-matching — actual reasoning over context)
3. **ACT** — Executes tasks: posts entries, sends communications, triggers workflows
4. **LEARN** — Improves over time based on outcomes and human feedback

**Key Differentiators Table:**

| | RPA Bot | AI Copilot | AI Agent |
|--|---------|------------|----------|
| Trigger | Scheduled/manual | User prompt | Autonomous/event-driven |
| Decision-making | None (scripted) | Suggests to human | Decides within policy |
| Scope | Single task | Single interaction | End-to-end process |
| Exception handling | Fails/escalates | Suggests fix | Resolves or escalates intelligently |
| Example | Auto-post JE at month-end | "Draft this accrual entry" | Detects missing accrual → calculates → posts → routes for approval |

> *Speaker Note: The agent doesn't wait for you to ask. It senses an event (e.g., bank statement arrives), reasons through the reconciliation, acts on matches, and escalates only the exceptions. The human shifts from "doer" to "governor."*

### **SLIDE 11 — Deep Dive: The Finance Agents** *(3 min)*

**Headline:** Inside the Agents Powering Autonomous Finance

**Agent 1: Cash Management Agent**
- **What it does:** Reasons over daily bank statements, auto-reconciles, recommends cash positioning
- **Impact:** Up to **70% time savings** on manual cash positioning tasks
- **How it works:** Ingests bank feeds → matches against GL → flags exceptions → proposes positions → learns from corrections

**Agent 2: Dispute Resolution Agent**
- **What it does:** Matches invoices against POs, identifies root causes of mismatches, communicates with vendors, resolves disputes within thresholds
- **Impact:** Compresses dispute cycles from **weeks to hours** for standard cases
- **How it works:** Invoice received → auto-match to PO → discrepancy detected → root cause analysis → vendor communication → resolution or escalation

**Agent 3: Accounting Accruals Agent**
- **What it does:** Calculates accruals and deferrals based on system data, presents proposals for accountant review
- **Impact:** Eliminates manual accrual calculations at period-end
- **How it works:** Period trigger → scans open commitments → calculates accruals → generates JE proposal → routes for approval

**Agent 4: International Trade Classification Agent**
- **What it does:** Classifies goods for international shipping, recommends customs tariff numbers and commodity codes
- **Impact:** Reduces manual classification errors and compliance risk
- **How it works:** Product master data → regulatory knowledge base → classification proposal → compliance check → approval routing

### **SLIDE 20 — Risks & Guardrails** *(2 min)*

**Headline:** What Can Go Wrong — And How to Prevent It

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| **Data Quality** — Agents amplify bad data at machine speed | Invest in master data governance BEFORE agent deployment |
| **Hallucination / Errors** — AI agents can make confident but wrong decisions | Implement approval thresholds; high-value actions require human sign-off |
| **Audit Trail** — Regulators need to understand who/what made a decision | Ensure all agent actions are logged with full explainability |
| **Over-Automation** — Automating processes that aren't well-understood | Map and standardize processes first; don't automate chaos |
| **Vendor Lock-In** — Deep dependency on single AI platform | Maintain interoperability; leverage open standards |
| **Change Management** — Finance teams resist or fear AI | Invest in training; frame as augmentation, not replacement |
| **Security & Compliance** — AI accessing sensitive financial data | SOX controls, GDPR compliance, access governance for agents |

**Key Message:** *"The biggest risk isn't that AI agents will fail. It's that you'll deploy them on broken processes and bad data, and they'll automate the wrong things at scale."*

> *Speaker Note: This slide builds credibility. Consultants who only sell the dream lose trust. Show you understand the risks. The data quality point always resonates — every finance team knows their data isn't perfect.*

## DESIGN GUIDELINES FOR THE PPT

### Color Palette
- **Primary:** SAP Blue (#0070F2) or your firm's brand
- **Accent:** Teal for AI/innovation, Orange for urgency/stats
- **Background:** White or very light gray — keep it clean

### Slide Design Principles
1. **One idea per slide** — no walls of text
2. **Visual-first** — use icons, timelines, comparison tables, not paragraphs
3. **Max 6 bullet points per slide** — if more, split
4. **Every stat has a source** — builds credibility
5. **Use callout boxes** for key quotes and critical numbers
6. **Consistent iconography** — use a single icon set (Phosphor, Lucide, or SAP Fiori icons)

### Animations (if using)
- Simple fade-in for sequential points
- No spinning, bouncing, or distracting transitions
- Use progressive reveal for the "Day in the Life" comparison slides

### Recommended Fonts
- **Headings:** 72Regular or your firm's brand font
- **Body:** Calibri, Inter, or SAP 72
- **Stats/Numbers:** Bold, oversized (36-48pt) for impact
